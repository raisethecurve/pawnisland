The Four Pillars of Decision-Making

I teach a decision-making method that transforms how you approach a chess position:

Observation

Elemental analysis of a position which informs evaluation. Use classical principles and modern insights to see what's truly important.

Evaluation

Form natural language opinions as the basis for candidate selection. Transform your earlier observations into actionable insights.

Three Candidates

The human mind is a difference engine which must be given multiple options to function.

Deduction

Learn to eliminate flawed candidates until one remains. Build up a robust repertoire of static and dynamic patterns to aid in the process.
