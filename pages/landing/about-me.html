About Me

Hi, I’m Matt—founder of Pawn Island, chess coach for over 20 years, poet at heart, and proud Rhode Island State Chess Champion (officially recognized by the Ocean State Chess Association for 2024). My journey with chess began as a child under the guidance of my mentor, Mike Foycik, who has helped shape my approach to the game for two decades. In 2003, I claimed the Virginia State Grade 12 Championship, and just a few years later, at age 20, I defeated my first International Master in a classical tournament—an experience that fueled my belief in the power of persistence and feedback.

My father once told me, “If you can master one thing, you can master anything.” I never truly understood what he meant until I immersed myself in chess. The process of mastery—this ongoing dance of trial, error, and reflection—taught me to understand my own mind in a focused environment. Once you master your thinking in one domain, the possibilities for growth and exploration become boundless.

I’ve had the privilege of coaching a wide range of students, from young beginners to varsity teams. In 2003, I guided two teams from Classical High School to nationals in Baltimore, and I love helping others discover the same joy of competition and self-discovery that chess has given me. Through it all, my supportive parents and my late brother, Tom, have been my biggest inspirations—Tom and I originally dreamed of creating an apparel brand that stood out from mainstream designs.

That dream lives on with Pawn Island, a line of artful, understated apparel for those who value self-expression over conformity. We launched in November, and in honor of our shared vision (and my faith), I donate 10% of net revenue to Waters Church in North Attleboro. Although I keep my journey with Jesus fairly subtle, my values of integrity, growth, and compassion guide everything I do.

Beyond the chessboard and apparel design, I’ve spent years writing poetry under the moniker raisethecurve on Tumblr, even becoming a “spotlit” blog and a tag editor on the platform. I still connect with my spoken-word community at Commonwealth Slam in Virginia and find creative expression to be as essential to my life as the next big chess tournament.

My goal is simple: to help people master their own minds, one move—or one verse—at a time. Whether you’re here for chess coaching, a fresh T-shirt design, or just a dose of inspiration, I’m grateful you’ve stopped by. Let’s make our next move our best move, together.

AntiFish Chess Engine Development

Role: Founding Member and Lead Data Analyst

Duration: 2018-2019

Project Overview:

AntiFish is a neural network-based chess engine developed by Cscuile, designed to identify and exploit weaknesses in traditional engines like Stockfish. Built upon the Leela Chess Zero (Lc0) framework, AntiFish aimed to enhance neural network performance against classical alpha-beta search engines.

AntiFish was Lc0, trained specifically to exploit weaknesses in Stockfish. AntiFish was trained from 96,308 games (As of AntiFish Mark 289) of Stockfish 11 Dev (77k Nodes) vs AntiFish Mark 25 and Leela ID 11258 (800 Nodes), with temperature. These games are then used to train off of ID 32425 with a heavy focus on policy, and a learning rate of 0.00002. (Up to Mark 125 Only)

Key Contributions:
  • Training Data Analysis: Conducted comprehensive analyses of training games to evaluate and compare the performance of successive engine generations, both statistically and analytically.
  • Synthetic Data Generation: Proposed and worked with lead engineer to implemented the innovative use of Stockfish to generate high-quality synthetic training data, enhancing the robustness of the neural network's learning process.
  • Collaborated with leading engineers and theoreticians to create new benchmarking test suites for chess engines.
  • Project Evolution: Played a pivotal role in the conceptualization and development of DarkQueen, a GPU-based chess engine forked from AntiFish, to further explore and validate advanced machine learning applications in chess.
  • Research Validation: Contributed to efforts aimed at replicating and extending the proof-of-concept experiments conducted by Google DeepMind with AlphaZero, focusing on the hypothesis that higher-quality data could outperform random initialization in neural network training.
Achievements:
  • Enhanced the performance and strategic capabilities of neural network-based chess engines through innovative training methodologies.
  • Advanced the understanding of synthetic data's role in machine learning, particularly within the domain of computer chess.
  • Contributed to the development of competitive chess engines that participated in prestigious tournaments, including Chess.com's Computer Chess Championship.
  • Finished clear 1st place in the CCC6 Winter Classic Stage 1 (5|2).
  • Finished clear 4th place in the CCC8 Deep Dive (15/5)
Technologies Used:
  • Neural Networks
  • Machine Learning
  • Python
  • TensorFlow/PyTorch
  • Chess Engine Frameworks (Lc0, Stockfish)

DarkQueen Chess Engine Development

Role: Founding Member and Lead Data Analyst

Duration: 2019–2020

Project Overview:

DarkQueen is a GPU-accelerated neural network chess engine developed to advance machine learning applications in chess. Evolving from its predecessors, AntiFish and BetaOne, DarkQueen aimed to validate the hypothesis that high-quality training data extracted from high-level (2400+) human games could enhance engine performance beyond traditional random initialization methods.

Dark Queen makes use of the Lc0 binary. It began as a project to observe how q-learning affects the value head of a network, and is still in its early development stages. It is currently trained completely using human online games.

Key Contributions:
  • Training Data Analysis: Conducted comprehensive analyses of training games to evaluate and compare the performance of successive engine generations, both statistically and analytically.
  • Benchmarking Development: Worked alongside engineers and theoreticians to create new benchmarking test suites for chess engines, facilitating more accurate performance assessments.
  • Project Evolution: Played a pivotal role in transitioning from CPU-based to GPU-based architecture, enhancing computational efficiency and enabling more complex neural network models.
  • Research Validation: Contributed to efforts replicating and extending Google DeepMind's AlphaZero experiments, focusing on the impact of high-quality data in neural network training.
Achievements:
  • Enhanced the strategic capabilities of neural network-based chess engines through innovative training methodologies.
  • Advanced the understanding of high-quality human data's role in machine learning within computer chess.
  • Developed a competitive chess engine that participated in prestigious tournaments, including Chess.com's Computer Chess Championship.
  • Achieved a peak rating of 3458.5 on Chess.com, reflecting significant advancements in engine strength.
Technologies Used:
  • Neural Networks
  • Machine Learning
  • Python
  • TensorFlow/PyTorch
  • Chess Engine Frameworks (Lc0, Stockfish)
