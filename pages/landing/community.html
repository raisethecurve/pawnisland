Frank DelBonis

OSCA President MACA Board Member NECA President RI USCF Delegate

A cornerstone of New England chess, Frank DelBonis has dedicated decades to promoting and organizing chess across multiple state affiliates. His leadership spans from local club management to representing Rhode Island at national USCF delegate meetings.

Biography

Frank DelBonis stands as one of New England's most influential chess organizers, with leadership roles spanning three major state chess affiliates. As President of the Ocean State Chess Association (OSCA), he has transformed Rhode Island's chess landscape, bringing innovative tournaments and growing membership across the state.

His involvement with the Massachusetts Chess Association (MACA) as a board member demonstrates his commitment to regional chess development beyond state borders. Frank's expertise in tournament organization and chess politics has made him an invaluable resource for chess communities throughout New England.

As President of the New England Chess Association (NECA), Frank works to coordinate efforts across all six New England states, fostering collaboration and shared resources among state affiliates. His vision for regional chess unity has led to numerous successful inter-state tournaments and collaborative initiatives.

Frank's role as a USCF delegate representing Rhode Island takes him to national meetings where he advocates for local chess interests and brings national perspectives back to the regional level. His diplomatic skills and deep understanding of chess governance make him an effective voice for New England chess at the national stage.

Alex Lumelsky

CSCA President NECA Board Member CT USCF Delegate Tournament Director

A driving force in Connecticut chess, Alex Lumelsky leads the Connecticut State Chess Association (CSCA) while serving as both a NECA board member and Connecticut's USCF delegate. His expertise in tournament organization and direction has elevated the quality and reach of chess events throughout Connecticut and New England.

Biography

Alex Lumelsky stands at the forefront of Connecticut's chess community as President of the Connecticut State Chess Association (CSCA). Under his leadership, the CSCA has expanded its reach and influence, bringing high-quality chess programming to players across the state. His vision for organized chess has transformed the Connecticut chess landscape through innovative tournament formats and comprehensive player development programs.

As a board member of the New England Chess Association (NECA), Alex contributes to the strategic direction of regional chess development across all six New England states. His collaborative approach helps coordinate resources and initiatives between state affiliates, fostering a unified vision for New England chess growth. This regional perspective enhances his effectiveness as both a state leader and national representative.

In his role as Connecticut's USCF delegate, Alex represents the state's interests at national chess meetings and policy discussions. His deep understanding of both local needs and national chess governance, informed by his regional NECA experience, allows him to effectively advocate for Connecticut players while bringing valuable insights back to the state level. His diplomatic approach and strategic thinking have made him a respected voice in national chess circles.

Alex's expertise as a tournament organizer and director is evident in the numerous successful events he has coordinated throughout Connecticut and the broader New England region. His attention to detail, player-focused approach, and ability to manage complex logistics have set new standards for tournament excellence. From local club tournaments to major state championships and regional events, his tournaments are known for their professional organization and welcoming atmosphere.
